# WebLLM Gateway Viral Video Design

## Style Prompt

Public hacker experiment with launch-film pressure. The video should feel like somebody connected Qwen 3.7 MAX web access to OpenClaw and Hermes, then proved lightweight tool calling is useful without overpromising complex coding. Use heavy type, warm darkness, rust-orange slices, proof-green terminal moments, and real UI evidence. The viral hook is direct; the explanation stays plain, practical, and legible.

## Colors

- `#120d0b` background: warm near-black.
- `#fff0db` foreground: warm paper white for headlines.
- `#d4501e` accent: rust-orange slashes, chips, transitions and CTA.
- `#ffb170` secondary accent: explanatory highlights.
- `#64ffa1` proof green: terminal success and tool evidence only.

## Typography

- Headlines: `Arial Black`, `Microsoft YaHei UI`, sans-serif, very heavy and compact.
- Captions/body: `Microsoft YaHei UI`, sans-serif, large and readable.
- Evidence/code: `Cascadia Code`, `Consolas`, monospace.

## Motion

- High-energy entrances: headlines slam, evidence panels snap, screenshot frames whip in.
- Reuse rust-orange wipe / diagonal slice transitions between scenes.
- Slow down during the real task proof so the viewer can read the evidence.
- Keep all major statements legible in silent playback.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not drown the video in protocol terms.
- Do not use purple-blue AI gradients or floating orb decoration.
- Do not show sensitive cookies, bearer tokens, API keys, or account details.
- Do not imply Gateway executes local tools or bypasses client permissions.
- Do not position Claude Code, Codex, or complex programming workflows as the main value.
- Do not replace real UI evidence with vague abstract particles.
